Timeline of Events

  • 5:45 a.m.: Hijackers pass through Portland, Maine airport security.
  • 6:00–7:40 a.m.: Hijackers board four flights.
  • 8:46 a.m.: Flight 11 crashes into the North Tower (WTC 1).
  • 9:03 a.m.: Flight 175 crashes into the South Tower (WTC 2).
  • 9:37 a.m.: Flight 77 crashes into the Pentagon.
  • 10:03 a.m.: Flight 93 crashes in Pennsylvania after passenger resistance.
  • 10:28 a.m.: North Tower collapses.
  • 5:20 p.m.: WTC Building 7 collapses.

The Planes Involved

  • Flight 11: Boston to L.A. (Boeing 767‑200ER)
  • Flight 175: Boston to L.A. (Boeing 767‑200ER)
  • Flight 77: Dulles to L.A. (Boeing 757‑200)
  • Flight 93: Newark to San Francisco (Boeing 757‑200)

Reaction and Aftermath

That evening, President Bush addressed the nation: “Terrorist attacks can shake the foundations of our biggest buildings, but they cannot touch the foundation of America.”

On September 12, NATO invoked Article 5. On October 7, the U.S. launched Operation Enduring Freedom in Afghanistan. In May 2011, Osama bin Laden was killed in a Navy SEAL operation.
